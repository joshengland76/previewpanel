# Welcome to PreviewPanel

Thanks for helping test this out.

**Your invite code.** [INVITE CODE] — enter this the first time you open
PreviewPanel. It's tied to you; if you clear your browser or switch
devices, just enter the same code again to get back in.

**What it does.** Upload a video before you post it, and a panel of AI
judges scores it — hook strength, pacing, how it's likely to land — and
gives you a percentile: how it compares to your own typical video, and to
everyone else's. It's a second opinion before you hit publish, not a
guarantee.

**Why connecting your TikTok matters.** The score comes from a prediction
model, and the only way to know if a prediction model is any good is to
check it against what actually happens. When you connect your TikTok, we
check our predictions against your real, publicly posted videos — that's
the whole validation loop, and it's the main reason we're doing this test
batch at all. If nobody connects their real results, we're just guessing
whether the model works.

**Exactly what we collect.** Your public TikTok posts (the video itself,
plus whatever engagement counters TikTok shows publicly — views, likes,
comments, shares, saves) for videos you post after connecting. Nothing
private, nothing beyond what anyone visiting your profile could already
see. We match your posted videos back to the previews you uploaded here
using a fingerprint of the video itself (not your account activity) — so
we can tell "this preview became that real post," and see whether our
score called it correctly.

**What the score means — and doesn't.** It's a ranking aid, not a crystal
ball. It tells you which of your own videos is likely to be your stronger
one, based on real 30-day engagement data from ~4,900 videos across 259
creators. It doesn't promise a specific outcome, doesn't tell you a video
will "go viral," and it isn't causal advice — if you see a note about video
length, that's a described pattern in the data, not an instruction to make
your videos longer or shorter. Some content categories don't have enough
reliable data yet to score numerically; for those we'll say so plainly
instead of making up a number.

**Cost, and what happens later.** Free for the whole beta — no card,
no charge. Each tester gets a monthly allowance of submissions, shown
right in the app ("Beta allowance: X of Y left this month"); if you run
out, we'll say so and point you to the feedback channel below rather
than leave you guessing. PreviewPanel will be a paid product at launch —
testers get founding terms for helping us get here.
